01 Getting Started

Installing the App

The ASH Inspection App is distributed as an Android APK file. You will receive this file directly from your manager. The app is not available on the Google Play Store.

1
Enable unknown sources On your Android device, go to Settings → Security (or Settings → Apps → Special App Access → Install Unknown Apps) and allow installation from your file manager or email app.
2
Open the APK file Open the APK file you received (via email or file transfer) and tap Install when prompted.
3
Launch the app Once installed, the app will appear on your home screen or app drawer as ASH Inspections.
Note
When a new version of the app is released, you will receive an updated APK. Simply install it over the existing app — your data and login are preserved.

Creating Your Account

Each Property Manager registers once using the app. Only names pre-approved by the administrator appear on the registration screen.

1
Tap "Create an account" on the sign-in screen.
2
Select your name from the list of approved Property Managers.
3
Enter your email address — use whichever address you prefer to receive reports on. This becomes your login username.
4
Choose a password of at least 8 characters, then confirm it.
5
Check your inbox — a confirmation email will arrive. Click the link inside to activate your account.
6
Return to the app and sign in with your email and password.
Important
You must click the confirmation link in your email before you can sign in. If you do not receive the email within a few minutes, check your spam folder.

Signing In

Enter the email address and password you registered with. The app will keep you signed in between sessions unless you explicitly sign out. If you forget your password, tap Forgot password? on the sign-in screen.

02 The Property List

After signing in, you will see a list of all properties assigned to you. Properties belonging to other Property Managers are not shown.

Navigating the List

  • Properties are listed alphabetically by name.
  • Tap a property to view its details and start or resume an inspection.
  • Use the search bar at the top to filter by name or address.

Reporting an Issue

If you encounter a problem with the app itself — a bug, an unexpected error, or a suggestion for improvement — tap Report issue in the top-right corner of the property list screen. A short description is all that is needed. The report is sent directly to the administrator and does not affect any inspection data.

Signing Out

Tap the sign-out icon in the top-right corner of the property list screen. You will be returned to the sign-in screen. Any locally saved inspection data is retained on the device.

03 Conducting an Inspection

Starting an Inspection

1
Select the property from your property list.
2
Tap "Start Inspection". The timer will begin and the inspection screen will open.
3
Work through the property recording observations and photographs as you go. There is no fixed order — move through the building naturally.
Tip
The screen will remain on throughout the inspection. The app will keep you logged in and active so recordings are never lost mid-inspection.

Recording Observations

Observations are recorded by voice. Each voice recording becomes one item in the inspection report.

1
Tap and hold the microphone button to begin recording. Release to stop.
2
The app transcribes your recording and uses AI to classify it into the correct inspection section (e.g. Grounds, Stairwells, Roof). A confidence indicator shows how certain the classification is.
3
Review and confirm the section. If the AI has placed it in the wrong section, tap the section name to correct it manually.
Narration Tips
  • Speak clearly and at a normal pace — the AI handles accents well but clarity always helps.
  • Describe what you can see, not just that something is present. For example: "There is a crack approximately 300mm long running horizontally across the render on the south elevation" rather than "there's a crack."
  • Include the location in your narration — "on the first floor landing", "adjacent to the bin store entrance". The AI uses this context to place the observation in the right section.
  • Each recording should cover one distinct issue or area. Avoid combining unrelated observations in a single recording — the AI will flag recordings that span multiple sections and ask you to split them.
  • You do not need to state the section name — the AI determines this from context.

Taking Photographs

Photographs are taken within the inspection screen using the camera button. The app automatically analyses each photograph using AI and associates it with the correct inspection section.

Important — Photo Sections
Always take photographs while physically in the relevant area. If you are standing in the stairwell, the photograph should be of the stairwell — not of something in an adjacent area. The AI classifies photos based on what it can see in the image. If a photo is placed in the wrong section, you can reassign it manually by tapping the section label on the photo card.

Duplicate Photos

If the app detects that three or more photographs have been taken within a two-minute window, it will display a warning banner highlighting potential duplicates. Review the photos and delete any that are redundant using the × button on each thumbnail, or the Delete button in the fullscreen viewer.

Inspection Sections

The app divides every inspection into twelve standard sections. See Section 9 of this manual for a full reference. Sections that are not applicable to a property (for example, a property without a car park) can be permanently marked as N/A — the app will remember this for all future inspections at that property.

04 Offline Working

The ASH Inspection App is designed to work in areas with poor or no mobile signal. All inspection data is saved locally on your device as you work.

What Works Offline

  • Recording and saving voice observations
  • Taking and saving photographs
  • Reviewing observations already recorded in this session
  • Continuing and ending an inspection

What Requires a Connection

  • Transcription — converting your voice recording to text requires a server connection. If you are offline when you record, the audio is saved to the device and transcribed automatically as soon as a connection is restored.
  • Photo analysis — AI classification of photographs happens in the background after the inspection, once your device is back online.
  • Report generation — the final report must be generated whilst connected.
Offline Indicator
A red banner at the top of the inspection screen indicates that the device is offline. Voice recordings made whilst offline will show a pending status. These are uploaded and transcribed automatically — no action is required from you. The banner clears once the connection is restored and all pending items have been processed.
Best Practice
Where possible, complete your inspection on-site and return to an area with mobile signal before generating the report. This ensures all photographs have been analysed and all transcriptions are complete before the report is compiled.
05 Completing an Inspection

Ending the Inspection

1
When you have finished recording all observations and photographs, tap End Inspection.
2
The app will sync your data to the server. A progress indicator is shown during this process. Do not close the app during syncing.
3
The Pre-Report Checklist will open automatically once syncing is complete.

Pre-Report Checklist

The checklist gives you a final overview of all twelve inspection sections before the report is generated. Each section is shown with a status indicator:

IndicatorMeaning
✅ GreenThe section has observations or photographs recorded.
🔵 BlueThe section has photographs only (no voice narration). The AI will synthesise an observation from the photos.
⚠️ AmberThe section has no observations and no photographs. Review whether this section applies to the property.
N/AThe section has been marked as not applicable. Toggle N/A using the button on each row.

Marking a Section as N/A

If a section is amber and genuinely does not apply (e.g. there are no lifts), toggle N/A on that row. For sections such as Car Park and Lifts, the app will ask whether this section is permanently not applicable to the property. Selecting "No, never" saves this setting — the section will be pre-marked N/A on all future inspections at this property. Selecting "Just this once" applies N/A only to the current inspection.

Going Back to Edit

If you notice a section that needs attention, tap the Edit button on that row. This returns you to the inspection screen with that section in focus, allowing you to add further observations or photographs before returning to the checklist.

Generating the Report

1
When the checklist is satisfactory, tap Generate Report.
2
The app processes your inspection — observations are refined, an overall summary is generated, and recurring issues from the previous inspection are identified. This typically takes 30–90 seconds.
3
The completed report is emailed to you automatically as both a Word document and a PDF. The email arrives at the address you registered with.
What the Report Contains
  • Property and inspection details (date, time, inspector, weather)
  • Overall condition summary
  • Observations by section, each with risk level and recommended action
  • Recurring items carried over from the previous inspection
  • Photographic appendix
  • Declaration and signature block
06 Tips for Best Results

Narration

Be Specific and Descriptive

The quality of the final report is directly related to the quality of your narration. The AI processes your words into professional report language, but it can only work with the detail you provide. Measurements, locations, severity, and context all improve the output significantly.

Instead of…Try…
"There's some damp on the wall.""There is a damp patch approximately 400mm in diameter on the internal face of the north wall on the ground floor communal corridor, adjacent to the entrance lobby."
"The lights aren't working.""Two of the four fluorescent light fittings on the second floor landing are not operational."
"The car park needs sweeping.""The car park surface has a significant accumulation of leaf debris and litter, particularly in the north-east corner adjacent to the boundary wall."
"Graffiti on the wall.""There is graffiti on the east-facing rendered wall of the bin store, approximately 1 metre in width and 0.5 metres in height."

One Issue Per Recording

Record each distinct defect or observation separately. If you combine multiple issues in one recording, the AI will attempt to split them — but separate recordings are cleaner and produce better results.

State the Action if Known

If you already know what action is needed, say it. For example: "This will require a contractor to inspect and re-point the brickwork." The AI will use this to populate the action column in the report.

Photography

Take Photos in the Right Location

The AI classifies photographs based on visual content. Always photograph the subject relevant to the section you are currently inspecting. A photograph taken in the bin store area should show the bin store — not an adjacent wall that belongs to a different section.

One Photo Per Issue

For each defect, one clear photograph is usually sufficient. Multiple photos of the exact same thing from the same angle add no value and may trigger the duplicate warning. However, multiple angles of a complex defect (e.g. a large crack photographed close-up and then in context) are helpful.

Photo Quality

  • Ensure adequate lighting — flash or natural light where possible.
  • Hold the phone steady and allow a moment for the camera to focus.
  • Step back to provide context where the defect is part of a larger area.
  • Landscape orientation works well for elevation shots; portrait for close-ups.

Mixing Narration and Photos

For best results, use both narration and photographs together. A narration without a photo is valid, and a photo without narration is valid (the AI will synthesise an observation from the image), but where a defect is significant, both together produce the most thorough report entry.

Photo-Only Inspections

It is possible to conduct an inspection using photographs only, without recording any voice narrations. In this mode, the AI will analyse each photograph, determine which section it belongs to, and automatically write a professional observation for inclusion in the report.

Strongly Recommended — Use Narration Alongside Photos

Photo-only inspections produce a usable report, but the AI is limited to what it can visually observe in the image. It cannot know the full context of a defect, how long it has been present, whether it has worsened, or what specific action you intend to recommend.

Reports generated from photos alone are likely to require significantly more editing before they are ready to send to a client. Voice narrations take only a few seconds per observation and result in a far more accurate and detailed report. Narration is always the preferred method.

If you do conduct a photo-only inspection, the pre-report checklist will show a blue indicator for sections covered by photographs only. You will still be able to add narrations via the Edit button before generating the report if you wish to supplement the photos.

General Workflow

  • Work systematically. Move through the property section by section to avoid missing areas. The app's inspection sections follow a logical exterior-to-interior order (see Section 9).
  • Record as you go. Do not wait until you return to the office — record observations on the spot while the detail is fresh.
  • Check the checklist carefully. Before generating the report, review each amber section on the pre-report checklist and confirm that missing sections are intentional.
  • Allow time for the report. Report generation takes up to 90 seconds. Stay on the screen until you see the confirmation message.
07 Admin Dashboard

The Admin Dashboard is a web-based monitoring tool for use by the firm's management. It is not part of the Android app and is accessed via a web browser.

Accessing the Dashboard

Open a web browser and navigate to:

https://ash-inspection-app-production.up.railway.app/admin

You will be prompted for a username and password. Enter the administrator credentials provided to you separately. The dashboard is not accessible without these credentials.

Dashboard Tabs

TabWhat It Shows
Live InspectionsAny inspection currently in progress — who is conducting it, which property, start time, and how long they have been on site. Refreshes automatically every 30 seconds.
Recent InspectionsThe last 25 completed inspections, with links to download the PDF report where one has been generated.
InspectorsAll registered Property Managers, their email addresses, job titles, and the date of their last inspection.
PropertiesThe full property portfolio with address, number of units, managing PM, and property features (car park, lift, roof access).
Bug ReportsAll issues and suggestions submitted by PMs via the in-app report button.
Costs & UsageAI API usage and cost tracking — total spend, breakdown by service and endpoint, monthly trends, and a full call log. A CSV export is available for financial reporting.

Summary Statistics

Across the top of the dashboard, six summary cards show at a glance: total properties, registered inspectors, inspections currently active, inspections completed this month, reports generated to date, and bug reports submitted.

Downloading a Financial Report

On the Costs & Usage tab, click Download CSV to export a full log of all API calls with timestamps, token counts, audio durations, and cost in USD. This file can be opened in Excel for financial reporting or expense reconciliation.

Note on Costs
API costs are charged in USD and are typically very small per inspection — a standard inspection with narration and photographs costs in the region of $0.05–$0.20 depending on the number of observations and photos. These are third-party charges from Anthropic (AI) and Deepgram (speech-to-text).

09 Inspection Sections Reference

The app divides every inspection into twelve standard sections. They are listed below in the order they appear in the report, which broadly follows an exterior-to-interior walkthrough.

#SectionWhat to Record
1 External Approach & Entrance Pathways, steps, ramps, gates, signage, lighting, and the main entrance door and surround approaching the building.
2 Grounds & Landscaping Condition of lawns, planting, boundary walls and fencing, drainage gullies, litter, and any external site furniture.
3 Bin Store & Waste Facilities Bin store structure, doors, cleanliness, bin provision, recycling facilities, and any fly-tipping.
4 Car Park Surface condition, line markings, lighting, drainage, barriers, signage, and security. Mark N/A if the property has no car park.
5 External Fabric & Elevations Condition of the building's external walls, render, cladding, windows, doors, guttering, downpipes, and soffits.
6 Roof Visible roof covering, flashings, chimney stacks, parapet walls, rooflights, and any plant equipment at roof level. Record from ground level or from roof access if available.
7 Communal Entrance & Reception Internal entrance lobby, entry system, post boxes, noticeboards, flooring, walls, ceiling, lighting, and fire door.
8 Stairwells & Circulation All internal common stairs, landings, balustrades, handrails, flooring, walls, ceilings, lighting, and fire doors on each level.
9 Lifts Lift car condition, doors, controls, certification, and any faults reported. Mark N/A if the property has no lift.
10 Plant Room & Utilities Boiler room, water tanks, electrical intake, meter cupboards, fire alarm panel, and any communal plant or equipment.
11 Meter Reads Record electricity, gas, and water meter readings. Photograph each meter display clearly so the reading is legible — these images are sent to the energy broker. Note the meter serial number if visible. Mark N/A if meters are not accessible during the inspection.
12 Internal Communal Areas Any communal indoor areas not covered above — corridors, common rooms, laundry rooms, storage, cycle stores, etc.
13 Additional / Property-Specific Anything unique to the property that does not fit the sections above — for example a private garden, a concierge facility, or a commercial unit.
Tip
Not every section will be relevant to every property. Use N/A for sections that genuinely do not apply rather than leaving them blank. This ensures the checklist is accurate and the report is not padded with empty sections.
